# Policies and Procedures Compliance Workflow Demo Guide

This guide provides a clear, structured walkthrough of the **Policies and Procedures Compliance Workflow**, summarizing key actions, roles, and best practices for demonstrating the workflow during a product demo.

---

## 📘 Overview

The **Policies and Procedures Compliance Workflow** ensures alignment with IDEA requirements by guiding users through policy review, findings management, and correction activities.

**Roles involved:**
- **Program Manager** – sets up the process and reviews findings  
- **Step Coordinators / Reviewers** – perform the policy review and manage assigned tasks  

**Key outcomes:**
- Identify non-compliance  
- Document findings  
- Assign corrective actions  

---

## 🚪 Accessing the Workflow

1. Navigate to **My Work → Tasks**  
2. Locate and open the **Policies and Procedures Review** task  
3. On the **Getting Started** page, select **Get Started**  
4. LEA team leaders and members see identical tasks; only assignment status differs  

---

## 📝 Performing the Review

### 🔹 Assigning and Starting
- Assign the review to yourself to become the **Team Leader**, or delegate to designate a **Team Member**  
- Select **Action → Start Review** to launch the compliance questionnaire  

### 🔹 Answering Compliance Questions
Each question must be marked as:
- **Compliant**
- **Non-Compliant**
- **Non-Applicable**

For any non-compliant or non-applicable response, a **comment explaining the rationale** is required.

### 🔹 Tracking Progress
- Use the navigation bar to move between sections  
- Completed sections display a **green checkmark**  

---

## 🗂 Managing Findings

On the **Summary** page:
- View all non-compliant items organized by category and legal citation  
- Add context or extenuating remarks in the comments field  
- Mark the review as **Complete** to forward it to the program manager  

---

## 🧑‍💼 Program Manager Review

Program Managers access a **Policies and Procedures Getting Started** task to review submitted findings.

They can:
- Review non-compliant items and open full details  
- Generate and customize correction activities using the built-in text editor  
- Edit the findings letter template and summary grid to formally document required actions  

---

## ✅ Finalizing and Next Steps

Once correction activities are drafted:
- Mark them as **Complete** to notify the district  
- Ensure the district receives assigned tasks and begins implementation  

---

## ⭐ Best Practices

- Answer every compliance question thoroughly and accurately  
- Provide detailed comments to justify non-compliant designations  
- Tailor correction plans to district context using the text editor tools  
- Monitor progress regularly and follow up promptly on outstanding corrections  

---

These talking points help keep your demo focused on critical workflow steps and highlight how the system drives IDEA compliance from review through resolution.
