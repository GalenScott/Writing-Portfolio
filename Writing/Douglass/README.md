## Top 10 Key Questions for Students

1. Why was literacy such a powerful tool for Frederick Douglass?  
2. How did Douglass continue learning after Sophia Auld stopped teaching him?  
3. What risks did Douglass and Anna Murray take during his escape?  
4. Why was Douglass’s autobiography so influential in the abolitionist movement?  
5. How did Douglass’s experiences with Edward Covey shape his views on resistance?  
6. What role did William Lloyd Garrison play in Douglass’s activism?  
7. Why did Douglass flee to Britain after publishing his autobiography?  
8. How did Douglass contribute to the Underground Railroad?  
9. What themes appear in Douglass’s letter to Thomas Auld?  
10. How did Douglass advocate for equality beyond abolition (e.g., women’s rights, education)?
